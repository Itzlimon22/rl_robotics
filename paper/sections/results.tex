\subsection{Ablation: Which Physics Parameter Drives the Gap?}
\label{sec:ablation}

Table~\ref{tab:ablation} presents results when only one physics parameter
is randomised at a time under Uniform DR, with all others fixed at
nominal values.
Lateral drag randomisation alone achieves
$[FILL:abl_drag_sr]\% \pm [FILL:abl_drag_std]\%$ transfer success,
the highest single-parameter performance.
This is consistent with lateral drag's quadratic velocity dependence
and its direct coupling with water current: the combined effect of
elevated drag and current load produces the largest out-of-distribution
force.

Water current randomisation alone achieves
$[FILL:abl_current_sr]\% \pm [FILL:abl_current_std]\%$, the second
largest contribution.
Actuator efficiency ($[FILL:abl_eff_sr]\%$), added mass
($[FILL:abl_mass_sr]\%$), and buoyancy ($[FILL:abl_buoy_sr]\%$)
contribute smaller but non-negligible transfer benefits.

Randomising any single parameter outperforms Naive SAC
($[FILL:sr_naive]\%$), confirming that even partial DR is beneficial.
However, all single-parameter conditions fall below Uniform DR
($[FILL:sr_udr]\%$), demonstrating that parameters jointly cover
distinct aspects of the sim-to-real gap that no single parameter
captures alone.
